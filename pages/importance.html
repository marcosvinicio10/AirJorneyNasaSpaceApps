How to Interpret Globe Data

Learn to read the data that appears on the interactive globe and understand what each piece of information means for your health and well-being.

Weather Data

Temperature

What it is: Air temperature in degrees Celsius

How to interpret: High temperatures can intensify pollutant formation. Values between 20-25°C are ideal.

Humidity

What it is: Amount of water vapor in the air (in %)

How to interpret: Low humidity (< 30%) can irritate airways. Ideal between 40-60%.

Atmospheric Pressure

What it is: Weight of air above us (in hPa)

How to interpret: Low pressure can indicate storms. Normal between 1013-1025 hPa.

Air Quality

CO₂ (Carbon Dioxide)

What it is: Gas that contributes to the greenhouse effect (in ppm)

How to interpret: Above 400 ppm indicates pollution. Ideal below 350 ppm.

Ozone (O₃)

What it is: Gas that protects in the stratosphere, but is toxic at ground level (in ppb)

How to interpret: Above 70 ppb is dangerous. Ideal below 50 ppb.

PM2.5 (Fine Particles)

What it is: Particles smaller than 2.5 micrometers (in μg/m³)

How to interpret: Above 25 μg/m³ is harmful. Ideal below 15 μg/m³.

Alerts and Colors

Green - Excellent

Meaning: Ideal air quality for all activities

Action: Can exercise outdoors without worry

Yellow - Moderate

Meaning: Acceptable air quality, but sensitive people may have problems

Action: Reduce intense outdoor activities

Red - Dangerous

Meaning: Air quality harmful to health

Action: Avoid outdoor activities, use mask if necessary
